class dynamicAll.base.CompositePotential(*potentials)[source]#

Bases: JaxPotential

Composite potential class that combines multiple potentials into a single potential.

There are two ways to combine potentials:

  1. Add potentials using the ‘+’ operator.

  2. Use the CompositePotential class to combine multiple potentials.

Functions Here just sum the density, mass, and potential of each potential in the composite potential.

Convert cartesian coordinates \((x,y,z,vx,vy,vz)\) to spherical coordinates \((r,\theta,\phi,v_{r},v_{\theta},v_{\phi})\)

classmethod dF_helper(eps, r_e, params)[source]#

Eddington formula for the distribution function of a spherical isotropic system. This is using the standard variable names and definitions from Binney & Tremaine (2008) where \(\psi(r) = -\Phi(r) + \Phi_{0}\) and \(\mathcal{E} = \psi - \frac{1}{2}v^{2}\). In most cases we’ll take Phi_{0} = 0 in which case \(\mathcal{E}\) is the binding energy.

\[f(\mathcal{E}) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{8}\pi^2}\int_{r_\mathcal{E}}^{\infty} \frac{k(r)dr}{\sqrt{\mathcal{E} - \psi(r)}}\]

where k(r)

\[k(r) = \frac{r^{2}}{GM(r)}\left[\rho''(r)\ + \rho'(r)\left(\frac{2}{r} - \frac{4\pi r^{2}\rho(r)}{M(r)}\right)\right]\]

See the documentation for more details on the derivation and tests.

jFactor(theta, d, rt)[source]#

J-factor

where we write r as \(r^2=\ell^2+d^2-2 \ell d \cos \theta\) where \(\ell\) is the distance along the line of sight and d is the distance to the center of the galaxy. Spherical symmetry lets us write \(d\Omega=2\pi\sin\theta d\theta\).

The bounds on the line of sight are $ell_{ pm}=d cos theta pm sqrt{r_{200}^2-d^2 sin ^2 theta}$. where $r_{200}$ is the ‘size’ of the system.

substituting in the integral we get $$ J(theta_{rm max}) = 2 pi int_{0}^{theta_{rm max}} int_{ell_{-}}^{ell_{+}} left[rholeft(sqrt{ell^2+d^2-2 ell d cos theta}right)right]^{2}sin(theta) dell dtheta $$
